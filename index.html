Federated learning in audio processing introduces several unique challenges that warrant thorough exploration. First, data heterogeneity across devices can lead to audio quality and content inconsistencies, affecting the reliability of models trained in such decentralized environments. The constraints of varying computational resources and storage capacities on different devices further complicate model training and efficacy. Maintaining privacy while managing large-scale audio data distributed among numerous devices also presents significant security concerns. Robust encryption methods are required to protect sensitive data during transmission. Moreover, the asynchronous nature of device connectivity can result in delays and discrepancies in model updates, undermining the learning algorithm's performance. Addressing these challenges necessitates the development of algorithms capable of accommodating data diversity and device variability, enhanced security protocols, and efficient strategies for connectivity and synchronization management.

In this workshop at ICASSP 2025, we aim to facilitate the sharing of cutting-edge research, foster collaborations among scholars, and drive forward the practical applications of federated learning in audio processing.
